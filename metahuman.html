MetaHuman Shakespeare AR Edition

Metahumans offer high-fidelity, photorealistic, fully rigged, and customizable digital avatars. However, full integration into AR and motion capture within XR technology remains underexplored. For my senior thesis (COGS 402), I conducted design research on Metahuman capabilities and limitations for XR immersive experiences, focusing on motion capture and augmented reality, as well as exploring virtual avatars and the interaction between Metahumans and digital humans.

This project stemmed from Shakespeare XR led by Dr. Patrick Pennefather. The objective was to utilize MetaHuman from Unreal Engine and fully integrate it with MOCAP technology to be used as a handheld AR tool for the Shakespeare XR project.

The process involved three main steps: Building MetaHuman MOCAP + MetaHuman AR + Metahuman

    • Built MetaHuman using MetaHuman Creator & 3D Mesh-to-MetaHuman

Process: MetaHuman Creation

We wanted to recreate the characters and the actors/actresses as MetaHumans. At the time of the project and program updates, I used MetaHuman Creator, working on preset MetaHuman to customize the characters and used the Unreal Engine MetaHuman Plug-in of conververting 3D mesh to MetaHuman.

For the 3D scan, I used the Polycam, brought that mesh into Blender to clean the mesh before importing it into Unreal to create the MetaHuman.

Process: MOCAP

In collaboration with Sawmill Studio, we used Optitrack Flex 13 system integrated with PeelCapture and Motive to capture motion. Post-Production With the collected data, I further cleaned raw motion captured data: removing jitters, correcting swapped markers, and filling in missing data. With the clean data, I solved/attached onto MetaHuman. The tool that was used was PeelSolve Autodesk Maya plugin.

Challenge:

I wanted to transfer the motion directly onto the MetaHuman using the PeelSolve tool. While the existing sources utilized live-streaming or retargeting, I wanted to utilize the advantage of post-production tool with cleaning the data using a proper MOCAP tool and maintaining as much the quality and data of the motion.

In doing so, I created custom mapping for solving. This is because the normal pipeline of solving is based on the default skeleton used for 3D models which have 4 spine joints, and 2 bones for arms, legs. However, the metahuman skeleton comes as two sets of skeleton with Body and face. With the body skeleton being more complex with 5 spine joints, extra corrective joints along the entire body.

Custom Solving Mapping

Process: AR development

Challenge: We wanted to make the MetaHuman integrated through web-AR. I went through different attempts of bringing MetaHuman outside of Unreal Engine. Though, in doing this, I also explored the HandHeld AR application.

  • Failed Builds for Web-AR (BlippAR & Aero)

For the Web-AR, I extracted the parts of MetaHuman are recompliled in Blender. The other method involved using the provided Maya file of MetaHuman. Though both mehthod resulted in loss of data, manual work in recreating or re-attaching each asset, not the same render quality as seen in Unreal Engine, and cases of loss of rigging or animation feature.

While the Handheld AR provided better resutls. I used the Unreal Engine 5.1 Handheld AR template. While it was possible to create MetaHuman in AR, there were some limitations.

In the initial builds, even with data compression of MetaHuman, there was too much data for mobile to render MetaHuman in AR resulting in:

  • Lag in the animation render
  • Failed to render complex shader maps
  • Had to recreate shaders (face, eyes, clothing) to render in textures
  • Couldn’t render high quality that helps with its realistic feature

Intital Builds through HandHeld AR

Through the project, this supported the MetaHuman's high-fidelity, quality and potential capabilities. With current capabilities of MetaHuman and it's development are well-supported for animation, rigging, MOCAP, and true-to-life motion emulation. Especially in it's pre-rigged feature with plenty new tools and programs that are released to support real-motion capture for MetaHuman. While my attempt focused on applying post-motion data onto the MetaHuman, this could be accelerated with the retargeting or live-streaming.

Though, for this projects objective, it did lack in it's rendering capacity through mobile due to its high quality and data. While it was too much to utilize MetaHuman in mobile AR, this could be further tested in developing a way to decrease data and increasing rendering capacity to bring digital avatars through handheld AR.
